# Remedy 02 vs Remedy 03: Owner-Friendly Comparison

## Purpose
A concise, business-focused comparison of Remedy 02 and Remedy 03 health insurance plans, using only confirmed, test-backed data. For owner and sales use—no technical or invented content.

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Remedy 02 and Remedy 03 share the same core annual limit, area of coverage, provider network, maternity structure, and most major rule foundations. The main practical differences are in outpatient copay, prescribed drugs, physiotherapy, and diagnostics / MRI. In day-to-day use, Remedy 03 is slightly stronger in some routine member-cost areas, while the overall plan structure remains very similar.

---

## 2. What is the Same in Both Plans
- **Annual Limit:** AED 150,000
- **Area of Coverage:** UAE, Indian Sub-continent, South East Asia (excluding Hong Kong & Singapore)
- **Provider Network:** HN Basic Plus (restricted outpatient clinic access, selected direct-access hospitals)
- **Inpatient Coverage:** All IP incl. surgery, covered in UAE and abroad
- **Outpatient Coverage:** General OP, Specialist OP, Diagnostics
- **Emergency Treatment:** Covered in UAE and abroad
- **Maternity Limit:** AED 10,000
- **Maternity Waiting Period:** Nil
- **Prenatal Services:** 8 visits, 10% copay, pre-approval required
- **Newborn Cover:** 30 days
- **Pre-existing Conditions:** 6-month waiting period, undeclared not covered
- **GP Referral:** Required for specialist consultation (clinics and hospitals)
- **Room and Board:** Prior approval required
- **Ambulance, ICU, Parent/Guardian Accommodation:** Covered
- **Annual Checkup:** Included
- **Optical, Alternative Medicine, Dental, Mental Health:** Identical limits and coinsurance

---

## 3. What is Different Between the Plans
| Feature                | Remedy 02                                 | Remedy 03                                 |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Outpatient Copay**   | 15% per visit                             | 10% per visit                             |
| **Prescribed Drugs**   | Up to AED 3,000/year, 30% copay, pre-approval | Up to AED 5,000/year, 20% copay, pre-approval, excess not covered |
| **Physiotherapy**      | 12 sessions/year, 15% copay               | 12 sessions/year, 10% copay, pre-approval |
| **Diagnostics / MRI**  | 15% copay, pre-approval                   | 10% copay, pre-approval                   |
| **Group Eligibility**  | Not specified                             | Applicable for groups size 21–50 lives    |
| **Dental, Optical, Mental Health** | Same as 02 (see above)                | Same as 02 (see above)                    |
| **Pre-existing / Disclosure** | Full disclosure required; 6-month exclusion applies; undeclared not covered | Full disclosure required; 6-month exclusion applies; undeclared not covered |

---

## 4. Practical Sales Interpretation
- For most members, the two plans are structurally very similar.
- Remedy 03 becomes more attractive mainly where frequent outpatient use, prescribed medication, physiotherapy, or diagnostics / MRI matter.
- Remedy 02 and Remedy 03 should not be presented as fundamentally different plan families; the main distinction is improvement in selected routine cost-sharing areas.
- Group applicability or commercial positioning should always be confirmed separately before quoting or presenting Remedy 03 as the preferred option.

---

## 5. Caution / Notes
- Do not oversell minor differences—core benefits are nearly identical.
- Both plans have a 6-month waiting period for pre-existing conditions and require full disclosure.
- All exclusions (e.g., dental prostheses, orthodontics, cosmetic, obesity, smoking cessation, contraception, elective vision correction, inpatient mental health except emergency, hearing/vision aids) apply equally.
- Always confirm group eligibility for Remedy 03.
- For benefits such as optical, alternative medicine, dental, mental health, or reimbursement inside UAE, confirm details with the latest policy or data before making any broad claims, as repo data may not fully specify all limits or eligibility.
